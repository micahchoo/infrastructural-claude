# First 30 Days Action Plan

## Week 1: Problem Validation Interviews

### Goal: Confirm the problem exists as you understand it

**Action items:**
1. Write a 10-question interview script focused on:
   - Current feedback workflow (walk me through your last week)
   - Worst experience with player feedback
   - Best experience where feedback led to a good decision
   - How they decide what NOT to build
   - What they'd pay for (open-ended, no anchoring)

2. Recruit 12-15 interviewees from:
   - r/gamedev, r/indiegaming (post asking for "devs in Early Access willing to chat about feedback workflows")
   - Indie dev Discord servers (Game Dev League, Indie Game Developers, etc.)
   - Your own network (5 years of game dev = you know people)
   - Twitter/Bluesky indie dev community

3. Conduct interviews (30 min each, recorded with permission)
   - Do NOT pitch your idea. Just listen.
   - Ask "show me" whenever possible — screen shares of their actual process

**Success criteria:** 8+ of 12 interviews reveal active pain with feedback triage. At least 3 describe workarounds that cost them 2+ hours/week.

## Week 2: Competitive Deep Dive + Workaround Mapping

### Goal: Understand what people are actually using today

**Action items:**
1. Sign up for and use: Canny (free tier), Nolt, FeatureUpvote, 3 Discord feedback bots
   - Document: setup time, first-day experience, what works, what's missing for games

2. Find 5 indie games with exemplary community management:
   - Study their Discord structure, how they handle feedback channels
   - Look for custom bots, Notion databases, Google Sheets, or other tools
   - Reach out to those devs — they're your early adopters AND design partners

3. Search for failed attempts:
   - Product Hunt graveyard for "game feedback" tools
   - Hacker News "Show HN" posts that didn't gain traction
   - Why did they fail? Wrong market, wrong approach, wrong timing, or wrong team?

**Deliverable:** A 2-page "competitive tear-down" — what exists, what's missing, and where you fit.

## Week 3: Concierge MVP

### Goal: Deliver the value manually before building anything

**Action items:**
1. Recruit 3 devs from your Week 1 interviews (offer it free, frame as "research collaboration")

2. For each dev:
   - Get read access to their Discord feedback channels (join as a member, or they share screenshots/exports)
   - Manually read 1 week of feedback
   - Synthesize into a structured digest:
     - Top 5 themes (with representative quotes)
     - Emerging trend (something gaining momentum)
     - Sentiment snapshot (overall mood, any red flags)
     - Your "if I were you" recommendation (1 thing to prioritize)

3. Deliver the digest and observe:
   - Did they read it?
   - Did it tell them something they didn't already know?
   - Did it change what they worked on?
   - What format/framing did they find most useful?

**Success criteria:** At least 2 of 3 devs say "this is genuinely useful" AND you can articulate what specifically was useful.

## Week 4: Decision Point + MVP Scoping

### Goal: Decide whether to build, and if so, scope the first version

**Action items:**
1. Synthesize everything:
   - Interview patterns
   - Competitive gaps
   - Concierge results
   - Your own conviction level

2. Answer these questions honestly:
   - Is the pain real and frequent? (not just "yeah that's annoying")
   - Are people actively solving this with workarounds? (willingness to invest effort = willingness to pay)
   - Can you deliver 80% of the concierge value with software? (if the value was in your judgment, not data, rethink)
   - Is this a tool or a service? (maybe the right product is a "feedback analyst as a service" not a SaaS)
   - Do YOU want to work on this for 2+ years?

3. If yes: write a 1-page product brief:
   - One sentence: what it is
   - One paragraph: who it's for and why they care
   - Feature list for v0.1 (Discord bot + dashboard, nothing more)
   - What's explicitly out of scope
   - First 3 design partners (from your interviewees)
   - Revenue goal for month 6 (suggest: 20 paying users = $300/mo = proof of life)

4. If no: you've invested 4 weeks and learned something real. That's not failure, that's due diligence.

## What NOT to Do in the First 30 Days

- **Don't write code.** Not a single line. Code is the most expensive way to validate an idea.
- **Don't design the UI.** You don't know what the right UI is yet.
- **Don't pick a tech stack.** That's a month-2 decision at earliest.
- **Don't build a landing page.** You don't have a value prop to test yet — you're still discovering it.
- **Don't talk to investors.** This is a bootstrap-first idea. Prove revenue before seeking money.
- **Don't incorporate a company.** Wait until you have paying users.

## Conversation Starters for Interviews

These are designed to avoid leading the witness:

1. "Tell me about the last time a player suggested something that actually made it into the game."
2. "How do you decide what NOT to work on when players are asking for it?"
3. "What's your process for reading feedback? Walk me through a typical session."
4. "Has there been a time when you realized too late that players were unhappy about something?"
5. "If you had a community manager for one day a week, what would you have them do?"
6. "What's the most surprising thing you've learned from player feedback?"
7. "Do you feel like you have a good sense of what your players want right now? How confident are you, 1-10?"
8. "Tell me about a feature you almost built because of player requests but decided not to."
9. "How do you communicate back to players about what you're working on?"
10. "What would have to be true for you to spend $15/month on a tool related to player feedback?"
