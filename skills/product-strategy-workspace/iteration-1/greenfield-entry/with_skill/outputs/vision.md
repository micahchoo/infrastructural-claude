# Product Vision — DevPulse

## Vision Statement

DevPulse helps indie game developers make strategic decisions about player feedback — not by tallying votes, but by connecting scattered community signals to the developer's own game vision. It aggregates feedback from Discord, Steam, and Reddit into a single hub, then layers on game-aware categorization and vision-alignment scoring so developers can confidently decide what to build, what to defer, and what to say no to. The result: developers spend less time reading and more time building the right things, while players feel heard even when the answer is "not now."

## Why This, Why Now

1. **The problem is universal and growing**: 13,000+ games ship on Steam annually. Every developer with an active community faces feedback fragmentation. The problem scales with market growth.

2. **Existing tools solve adjacent problems, not this one**: Voting boards (Canny) create entitlement. Analytics tools (SteamDB) are read-only. Project management tools (Jira) don't understand player feedback. The "strategic middle ground" between ignoring feedback and building everything is unserved.

3. **The founder has lived this problem**: Five years of game development provides deep domain empathy, community access for validation, and credibility with the target audience. This is a founder-market fit advantage that generic SaaS teams lack.

4. **Technical timing is right**: Discord bot APIs, Steam review access, Reddit API, and affordable NLP/LLM-based categorization make multi-platform ingestion feasible at indie-friendly cost for the first time.

## What This Product Is NOT

- **Not a voting board**: Players don't submit requests. The tool ingests organic feedback from where players already talk.
- **Not an analytics dashboard**: It doesn't just show what players say — it helps developers decide what to DO about it.
- **Not a community management platform**: It doesn't replace Discord moderation, ticketing, or player communication tools.
- **Not a project management tool**: It feeds INTO sprint planning tools, doesn't replace them.

## Core Bet

The central hypothesis is that indie game developers want a **feedback filter, not a feedback funnel**. They don't want more feedback surfaced — they want less noise and more signal. The product's value is in the subtraction (what can I safely ignore?) as much as the addition (what should I prioritize?).

If this hypothesis is wrong — if developers actually just want a voting board with better UX — then Canny wins and this product should not exist. Validate this assumption first.
