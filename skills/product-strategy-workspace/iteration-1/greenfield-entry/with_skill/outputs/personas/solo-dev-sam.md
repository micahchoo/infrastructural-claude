# Solo Dev Sam — The Passionate Solo Developer

## Behavioral Profile
- **Primary job**: Ship meaningful game updates that keep players engaged without burning out from community noise
- **Current tools**: Discord server (reads manually), Steam forum (skims weekly), a personal spreadsheet or Trello board to track "player requests I might do"
- **Pain points**:
  - Spends 5-10 hours/week reading feedback across 3+ platforms with no aggregation — time stolen from development
  - Cannot distinguish signal from noise: a vocal minority requesting PvP can drown out the silent majority who want bug fixes
  - Feels guilty ignoring feedback, but paralyzed when trying to act on all of it — leads to scope creep and delayed updates
  - No way to close the loop with players ("we heard you, here's what we decided") — erodes community trust
- **Workarounds**: Manually copies interesting suggestions into a spreadsheet; uses Discord reaction counts as a rough proxy for demand; occasionally polls players but gets biased samples from most-engaged users
- **Switch trigger**: A tool that saves 3+ hours/week on feedback triage AND helps them confidently say "no" to requests that don't align with their game's direction

## Design Implications
- Must be dead-simple onboarding — solo devs won't invest time learning a complex tool
- Aggregation across platforms is table-stakes, but the real value is the prioritization layer on top
- Needs to support "game vision" as a first-class filter — so the dev can evaluate feedback against their creative direction, not just popularity
- Communication back to players (roadmap sharing, "we heard you" signals) is a retention feature, not a nice-to-have
- Price sensitivity is high — this persona pays out of pocket, not a company budget

## Evidence
- [Source: founder domain expertise] — 5 years of game development experience observing this pattern firsthand
- [Source: market observation] — Steam developer forums and r/gamedev consistently surface "how do I handle player feedback" threads
- [Source: behavioral pattern] — indie devs on Discord commonly describe "feedback paralysis" where too much input leads to inaction
