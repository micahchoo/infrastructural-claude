# Onboarding Redesign: Week 2 Executive Summary

## Bottom Line

**It is too early to judge whether the onboarding redesign will hit its targets.** You are 33% through the 60-day evaluation window. The right question at Week 2 is not "is this working?" but "what does the trajectory look like, and what can we fix now?"

## What You Can Know Right Now

1. **Time-to-first-value (TTFV)** is your primary signal at this stage. This metric reflects single-session behavior and stabilizes quickly. Pull the median TTFV for post-launch signups from your analytics dashboard today. If it's at or near 15 minutes, the mechanical redesign is working.

2. **Trial-to-paid conversion cannot be reliably measured yet.** Conversion requires users to reach the end of their trial period. Unless you have very short trials (7 days) AND high volume, you don't have a meaningful sample yet. The activation rate (users completing key onboarding milestones) is the best available proxy.

3. **30 support tickets are a qualitative signal, not a statistical one.** Categorize them by type and onboarding step. Look for clustering -- if 10+ tickets mention the same step, that's an actionable finding. Compare the ticket rate to pre-launch to understand whether volume changed.

## Recommended Immediate Actions

| Priority | Action | Time Required | Output |
|----------|--------|---------------|--------|
| 1 | Pull TTFV median and distribution from dashboard | 30 min | Primary success metric |
| 2 | Categorize all 30 support tickets by type and step | 2 hours | Friction map |
| 3 | Pull onboarding funnel step-by-step drop-off rates | 30 min | Optimization targets |
| 4 | Compare Day-1 and Day-7 retention vs. pre-launch | 30 min | Stickiness check |
| 5 | Set Week 4 calendar reminder for conversion check | 5 min | Cadence discipline |

## What NOT to Do Right Now

- **Do not declare success or failure.** You don't have the data.
- **Do not redesign the flow again.** Fix specific broken steps, don't overhaul.
- **Do not report conversion numbers to stakeholders** unless you caveat them heavily with sample size warnings.
