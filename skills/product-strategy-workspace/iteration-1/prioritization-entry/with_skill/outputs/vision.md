# Product Vision — Pocketwise

## Vision Statement

Pocketwise is the personal finance app that adapts to how you actually think about money — not the other way around. For millennials who've bounced off rigid budgeting tools and fragmented financial workflows, Pocketwise provides a flexible, affordable, privacy-respecting financial home that grows from budgeting to household coordination to wealth visibility. We win by being the tool that solo users adopt for its simplicity and couples adopt for its collaboration — making every user a growth channel.

## Why Now

1. **Mint's death** left millions seeking alternatives; YNAB and Monarch captured many, but their rigidity/price creates ongoing churn
2. **Plaid commoditization** means a solo dev can offer the same bank connectivity as a funded startup
3. **Reddit-native distribution** gives authentic reach that money can't buy — but the window closes as competitors saturate these channels
4. **Millennial life stage**: The 28-40 cohort is simultaneously budgeting, coupling finances, and starting to invest — they need a tool that grows with them, not three separate apps

## Strategic Constraints

- **Solo dev reality**: Can only ship one major initiative per quarter. Sequencing is everything.
- **Revenue model**: Freemium with paid tier for household + investment features. Free tier must be genuinely useful (budgeting core) to maintain Reddit credibility.
- **Technical constraint**: Plaid costs scale with connected accounts. Investment tracking (brokerage connections) doubles Plaid costs per user. Must validate demand before committing.
- **Brand constraint**: "Indie, trustworthy, responsive" — any decision that undermines this (selling data, VC funding, ignoring feedback) is off the table.
