# Budget-Conscious Maya — The Intentional Spender

## Behavioral Profile
- **Primary job**: Maintain awareness of where every dollar goes so she can hit savings goals (emergency fund, vacation, debt payoff) without feeling deprived
- **Current tools**: Spreadsheet she built herself, bank app notifications, occasional use of Mint/YNAB (bounced off both — too complex or too rigid)
- **Pain points**:
  - Manually categorizing transactions is tedious; she falls behind and loses visibility (evidence: common Reddit r/personalfinance complaint pattern)
  - Existing tools assume one budgeting philosophy — she wants flexibility between envelope-style and percentage-based approaches
  - No good way to see "am I on track this month?" at a glance without opening multiple screens
- **Workarounds**: Weekly "money hour" where she reconciles her spreadsheet with bank statements; sets calendar reminders for bills
- **Switch trigger**: A tool that auto-categorizes accurately and shows a single "health score" for the month without requiring daily maintenance

## Design Implications
- Onboarding must demonstrate value in under 3 minutes — she's been burned by complex setup flows
- The core dashboard must answer "am I on track?" in one screen
- Auto-categorization accuracy is table-stakes; manual correction should be fast, not a workflow
- Flexible budgeting models (not one-size-fits-all) will retain her
- Bill reminders are a natural extension of her calendar-reminder workaround
