# Opportunity Framing — Pocketwise

## Opportunity Statements

### Opportunity 1: The Maintenance Budgeter
For **Budget-Conscious Maya**, who loses visibility into her spending because manual categorization is tedious and existing tools impose rigid methodologies, **Pocketwise provides flexible budgeting with smart auto-categorization and at-a-glance health scoring**, unlike YNAB (rigid envelope system) and Monarch (expensive, feature-bloated).

### Opportunity 2: The Household Coordinator
For **Coupled-Finances Jordan**, who fragments their financial life across three tools (budget app, Splitwise, shared spreadsheet) to coordinate household expenses, **Pocketwise provides a single financial home where shared AND personal budgets coexist with built-in settlement tracking**, unlike Splitwise (no budgeting), YNAB (no collaboration), and Monarch (basic sharing without asymmetric visibility).

### Opportunity 3: The Wealth Builder
For **Aspiring-Investor Alex**, who manually tracks net worth in spreadsheets because no budgeting app connects spending discipline to investment growth, **Pocketwise provides read-only investment visibility alongside budgets so users see the full financial picture**, unlike YNAB (deliberately excludes investments) and brokerage apps (no budgeting context).

## Compound Probability Assessment (bias:conjunction)

The full roadmap succeeds only if ALL of:
1. Bill reminders improve retention (probability: ~80% — well-understood feature, low risk)
2. Household budgets drive viral growth (probability: ~55% — collaboration features are hard to get right, and partner onboarding friction is real)
3. Paid tier achieves 200+ subscribers (probability: ~45% — willingness-to-pay for indie tools is uncertain; free alternatives exist)
4. Investment tracking demand persists after household launch (probability: ~60% — feature request may be from a vocal minority)

**Naive narrative probability**: "Of course this all works!" → feels ~80%
**Independent multiplication**: 0.80 * 0.55 * 0.45 * 0.60 = **~12%** for the full sequence

This is why the roadmap uses **conditional gates** rather than committing to all four. Each bet is evaluated independently. Failing at step 3 redirects to household deepening rather than pushing through to investment tracking.

## Known Gaps (bias:wysiati)

What we are NOT seeing because we haven't looked:
- **Users who churned**: We only hear from active users and feature requesters. The silent majority who left may have entirely different pain points
- **Non-Reddit users**: The acquisition channel is a single Reddit post. Users from other channels (organic search, word-of-mouth) may have different needs
- **International users**: Currency, tax, and banking integration differences could be a major segment we're ignoring
- **Accessibility needs**: No data on whether visually impaired or neurodiverse users can effectively use the current product
- **The "do nothing" users**: People who downloaded, connected a bank, and then never actively budgeted. What would activate them?

**Recommendation**: Before committing to Q3's household feature, invest 2-3 hours in exit survey instrumentation (part of Q2 analytics work) and 5 informal user interviews to pressure-test these gaps.
