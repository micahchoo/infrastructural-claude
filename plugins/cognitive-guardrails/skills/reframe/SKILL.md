# Reframe Check

Your conclusion is shaped by how you framed the problem at step one. "What could go wrong?" finds risks. "Is this ready?" finds reasons to say yes. Both use the same evidence.

Argue the opposite of your conclusion using the same evidence. What are the strongest reasons you're wrong? If the counter-argument is weak, proceed with confidence. If it's strong, you've caught a framing-dependent judgment — gather more evidence or flag the uncertainty explicitly.

`[eval: approach]` If counter-argument is stronger, switch rather than defend.
